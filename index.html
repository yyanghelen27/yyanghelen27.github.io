About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in the School of Mechanical Engineering at Purdue University, working on magnetic microrobots, two-photon polymerization, ultrasound-guided robotic systems, and biomedical applications.

My research focuses on developing magnetically actuated microrobots and image-guided robotic systems for minimally invasive diagnosis and therapy.

Research Interests

  • Magnetic microrobotics
  • Two-photon polymerization microfabrication
  • Embedded-magnet microrobots
  • Medical robotics
  • Ultrasound-guided magnetic navigation
  • Targeted drug delivery

Research Projects

Direct-Printed Embedded-Magnet Microrobots

This project develops a direct-printing strategy for integrating permanent micro-magnets into two-photon polymerized microrobots. The approach enables magnetic microswimmers, tumblers, and grippers with embedded magnetic components.

Hyper-Redundant Magnetic Snake Microrobots

This project explores modular snake-like microrobots for navigation in constrained biological environments. The design combines magnetic actuation with flexible or articulated microstructures.

Ultrasound-Guided Magnetic Navigation

This project focuses on robotic and ultrasound-guided navigation of magnetic microrobots for biomedical intervention and real-time tracking.
