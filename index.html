Research during Ph.D.

My Ph.D. research focuses on magnetic actuated microrobots. My goal is to develop microrobots and robotic platforms that can navigate complex biological environments for minimally invasive diagnosis and therapy.

* indicates equal contribution when applicable.

Direct-Printed Embedded-Magnet Microrobots
Yang Yang, Jeremy Gan, Jialong Huang, and David J. Cappelleri
Manuscript / ongoing Ph.D. research

Developed a direct two-photon polymerization strategy for integrating permanent micro-magnets into microrobots during fabrication. This approach enables multiple magnetic microrobot platforms, including helical microswimmers, tumblers, and grippers, while reducing post-print assembly complexity.

Hyper-Redundant Magnetic Snake Microrobots for Constrained Navigation
Yang Yang and David J. Cappelleri
Ongoing project

Designed modular snake-like microrobots with embedded magnetic components and compliant or articulated joints. The project explores reconfigurable locomotion and magnetic actuation strategies for navigation through confined biological environments.

Ultrasound-Guided Magnetic Microrobot Navigation
Yang Yang and collaborators
Ongoing project

Developed robotic and imaging workflows for ultrasound-guided magnetic microrobot navigation. This work combines robotic positioning, external magnetic actuation, real-time ultrasound imaging, and path-planning concepts for biomedical intervention.

Shape-Programmable Adaptive Multi-Material Microswimmers for Biomedical Applications
L. Tan, Yang Yang, L. Fang, and D. J. Cappelleri
Advanced Functional Materials, 2024
paper

Developed adaptive multi-material microswimmers with programmable shape change for biomedical microrobotics applications.

Sensor-Based Planning and Control for Conformal Deposition on a Deformable Surface Using an Articulated Industrial Robot
Yang Yang et al.
Journal of Manufacturing Science and Engineering, 2024
paper

Developed a sensor-based robotic planning and control framework for conformal deposition on deformable surfaces using an articulated industrial robot.
